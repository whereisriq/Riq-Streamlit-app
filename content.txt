Artificial intelligence is becoming an important part of modern education. 
Universities are increasingly using AI tools to support teaching, learning, 
and administrative processes. These tools can help students receive personalized 
feedback, assist instructors with grading, and improve access to learning resources.

However, the use of AI in education also raises concerns. Issues such as data privacy, 
academic integrity, and over-reliance on automated systems must be carefully managed. 
Institutions are responsible for ensuring that AI is used ethically and transparently.

When implemented thoughtfully, artificial intelligence has the potential to enhance 
learning experiences and improve educational outcomes. Universities must balance 
innovation with responsibility to ensure that AI supports, rather than replaces, 
human-centered education.
